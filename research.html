  • Akidul Haque, Pritam Saha, Shubhadeep Ghosh, Shahidul Islam, and Uttam Ghosh, "Effects of environmental fluctuation and Allee effect on predator of a Brazaikin-type population model." (Submitted).

    This study presents a modified predator-prey model incorporating key ecological factors: logistic growth of the prey population, Holling type II functional response for predation, and an Allee effect in the predator population and competition among the predators. This research contributes to ecological modeling by integrating deterministic and stochastic perspectives, providing insights for biodiversity conservation and ecosystem management.

  • MD Rakibul Hasan, Parker Frank, Saifur Rahman, Andrew Harrison, Sankar Chowdhury, and Akidul Haque, "Behavioural Interventions, Mental Health, and Microbial Dysbiosis as Intersecting Determinants of HIV PrEP Adherence: A Mixed-Methods Study Among African American Adults in the Southern United States." (Submitted).

    This study integrates behavioural, psychological, and microbiome perspectives to investigate HIV PrEP adherence among African American adults in the U.S. South. Using mixed-methods analysis, the work highlights how psychosocial stressors and microbial dysbiosis jointly impact adherence outcomes and intervention design.

  • Akidul Haque, Esrat Nur, Md. Anuar Hossen, and Shahidul Islam, "Dynamical Behavior Analysis of Anti-controlled Lorenz System." (Submitted).

    This work investigates the anti-controlled Lorenz system (Chen system), focusing on the role of control parameters in inducing or suppressing chaotic dynamics. Analytical and numerical methods are applied to reveal stability boundaries, bifurcation structures, and potential engineering applications of controlled chaos.

In Preparation

  • Dan Han, Akidul Haque, "Spatiotemporal Dynamic Lag Mod- els for Nowcasting COVID-19 Rates Using Waste Water Data."

    We are working on a Spatiotemporal Dynamic Lag models to newcast Covid-19 rates using wastewater data of different counties across New York state. This study incorporates a geographically weighted process to consider the heterogeneity of disease transmission and under-reporting in relation to wastewater virus concentration, the temporal lags to address virus incubation periods and reporting delays, and the spatial correlations across counties to enhance the prediction accuracy and early warning system.

  • Dan Han, Pamela Linares, Akidul Haque, "Relationship between wastewater virus levels and underreported positive COVID-19 cases."

    This ongoing study develops an ARCH-based modeling framework to examine the relationship between SARS-CoV-2 concentrations in wastewater and underreported COVID-19 cases. The work captures temporal volatility in case reporting, addresses under-ascertainment in surveillance data, and evaluates the predictive power of wastewater signals for enhancing outbreak monitoring and public health decision-making.

  • Akidul Haque, Md. Anuar Hossen, Md. Shahidul Islam, and Uttam Ghosh, "Mathematical analysis of controlling invasive species in competition with native species."

    In this paper, we aim to mathematically analyze the dynamics of invasive species competing with native species. The analysis explores interspecific interactions, equilibrium states, and stability conditions, with a focus on identifying threshold parameters that determine invasion success or extinction. The overarching aim is to propose effective mathematical strategies to mitigate invasive species spread while ensuring the persistence of native populations.
