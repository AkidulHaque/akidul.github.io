    • Akidul Haque and Dan Han, "Hierarchical Bayesian Elastic Net Methods on Exponential Random Graph Models."

      This work develops a hierarchical Bayesian framework integrating elastic net regularization into Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs) to address parameter sparsity, multicollinearity, and overfitting in complex network data. The proposed method enhances model stability and interpretability while enabling robust inference for high-dimensional social, epidemiological, and relational network structures.

    • Akidul Haque and Rafiqul Islam, "Two-strain Influenza Modeling in the United States."

      This study develops a comprehensive two-strain influenza transmission model that distinguishes between wild-type and antiviral-resistant strains circulating in the United States. By incorporating vaccination, co-circulation dynamics, mutation pathways, and real epidemiological data, the model examines reproduction numbers, endemic conditions, and long-term disease behavior. The findings offer insights for seasonal forecasting and inform public health decision-making on influenza mitigation strategies.

    • Akidul Haque, Md. Anuar Hossen, Md. Shahidul Islam, and Uttam Ghosh, "Mathematical analysis of controlling invasive species in competition with native species."

      In this paper, we aim to mathematically analyze the dynamics of invasive species competing with native species. The overarching aim is to propose effective mathematical strategies to mitigate invasive species spread while ensuring the persistence of native populations.
