About

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and an M.S. in Biostatistics as part of a dual-degree program at the University of Louisville. My research focuses on developing and applying advanced mathematical and statistical models to investigate complex systems, with particular emphasis on ecosystems and epidemic dynamics. By integrating theoretical analysis, statistical methodology, and computational simulations, my work seeks to uncover underlying mechanisms, quantify uncertainty, and bridge the gap between abstract theory and real-world applications.

I am currently working under the supervision of Dr. Dan Han on several projects in epidemic modeling, with a particular focus on under-reported cases. These projects include the development and application of models such as the Dynamic ARCH model and the Dynamic Lag model.

I am passionate about interdisciplinary collaboration, discussion, and continuous learning. Feel free to explore and don't hesitate to reach me.

Keywords: Data Driven Process Bayesian modeling Time series analysis Epidemiology Dynamical systems
