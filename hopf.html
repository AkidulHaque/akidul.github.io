Hopf Bifurcation in Nonlinear Dynamical Systems

Author: Akidul Haque | Published: September 2025

Objective

The objective of this project is to investigate the occurrence of Hopf bifurcations in nonlinear dynamical systems. A Hopf bifurcation is a critical phenomenon in which an equilibrium point loses stability, giving rise to sustained periodic oscillations. Due to the inherent complexity of nonlinear systems, providing an analytical proof of Hopf bifurcation can be challenging. Therefore, this project aims to present a detailed analysis of the occurrence and conditions of Hopf bifurcations.

Mathematical Framework

To determine if a system exhibits a Hopf bifurcation, we analyze the Jacobian matrix at the coexistence equilibrium and examine its eigenvalues. Typically, when a pair of complex conjugate eigenvalues crosses the imaginary axis, a Hopf bifurcation occurs.

Methodology

The analysis of Hopf bifurcation involves examining the stability of the coexistence equilibrium through eigenvalue analysis and applying explicit bifurcation theorems. The methodology consists of the following:

  • Hopf Bifurcation Explicit Theorem: The theorem introduces the first Lyapunov coefficient, denoted as \(a\), which determines the type of bifurcation:
    • When \(a > 0\): the bifurcation is subcritical, leading to unstable limit cycles.
    • When \(a < 0\): the bifurcation is supercritical, producing stable limit cycles.
    This analysis provides the criteria to identify whether the emerging periodic solutions are stable or unstable.

If solutions are shown to be positive and bounded, and the system does not converge to an equilibrium, then the trajectory must approach a closed periodic orbit.

Results

The analysis confirms that under certain parameter ranges, the first Lyapunov coefficient \(a < 0\), the coexistence equilibrium becomes unstable and a stable limit cycle emerges. This demonstrates the occurrence of a supercritical Hopf bifurcation with a periodic orbit bifurcating from the nontrivial equilibrium point.

Conclusion

Hopf bifurcations provide insight into the transition from steady states to oscillatory dynamics. This project highlights the role of eigenvalue analysis and nonlinear theory in understanding complex biological and epidemiological systems.
